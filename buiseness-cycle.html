Gross Domestic Product

  • The most wated statistic in macroeconomics is a nation's gross domestic product (GDP) - is defined as "The total value of all the goods and services produced by a country's economy in a year".

  • How fast or slow it is expanding (or if it is shrinking) reflects how well public policies are working. Tthere are three ways to calculate GDP, but we will only concern ourselves with the consumtion method.

  • GDP (Y) = C + G + I + NX, where
    • C = Consumption
    • G = Government
    • I = Investment
    • N = Net Exports
