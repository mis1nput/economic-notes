    Words & Definitions

    • Communism

      • a political theory derived from Karl Marx, advocating class war and leading to a society in which all property is publicly owned and each person works and is paid according to their abilities and needs.

    • Capitolism

      • an economic and political system in which a country's trade and industry are controlled by private owners for profit.

    • Command Econonomy

      • an economy in which production, investment, prices, and incomes are determined centrally by a government.

    • Competition

      • a scenario where different economic firms are in contention to obtain goods that are limited by varying the elements of the marketing mix: price, product, promotion and place.

    • Market Economy

      • a type of economic system where supply and demand regulate the economy, rather than government intervention.

    • Mixed Economy

      • an economy organized with some free-market elements and some socialistic elements

    • Private Property Rights

      • only a single user may possess the title and legal claim to the property.

    • Profit

      • the difference between revenue that an economic entity has received from its outputs and total costs of its inputs

    • Socialism

      • a political and economic system in which property and the means of production are owned in common, typically controlled by the state or government

    • Traditional Economy

      • a system that relies on customs, history, and time-honored believes
